The welfare and safety of the child comes first. If in any doubt, escalate — do not wait until morning.

What Constitutes a Concern

A child protection concern arises when you have reasonable grounds to believe a child (under 18) has been, is being, or is at risk of being: physically abused, emotionally abused, sexually abused, or neglected. Includes a direct disclosure, a concern raised by a GL or HF, or behaviour you observe.

What To Do — Follow In Order

  1. Listen carefully. Do not promise confidentiality. Do not press for details or ask leading questions.
  2. Do not confront or contact the person allegedly responsible for the harm.
  3. Ensure the student is in a safe location. If the concern involves the host family, consider activating the back-up host listed on the Booking Form.
  4. Contact Peter Lahiff immediately (number saved on phone). He is FL's designated child protection person.
  5. If child is in immediate danger and Peter is unreachable: call 999, then TUSLA: 0818 776 315.
  6. Make a written record — what was reported, by whom, and when. Email to Peter and Felipe before ending the call.

Moving Student to Back-Up Host

Student in Emotional Distress

  1. Stay calm. Ask the GL or HF to stay with the student.
  2. Establish whether the student is physically safe right now.
  3. Notify Felipe by phone or message. Do not treat this as next-day if the student's safety is unclear.
  4. YL minor: the GL is responsible for the student's immediate welfare and must remain with them.

Self-Harm or Suicidal Crisis

If a student has harmed themselves or is expressing intent to harm themselves:
  1. Call 999 immediately if the student is in immediate physical danger.
  2. Ensure someone is physically with the student at all times — do not leave them alone.
  3. Call Felipe or Peter Lahiff.
  4. Do not minimise, dismiss, or challenge what the student is saying.

What This Phone Is For

The emergency phone covers all FL programmes outside of office hours. Your goal is to resolve issues as far as possible without contacting other staff unless necessary. Most issues can and should wait until the next working day — triage, reassure, and document. Changes to accommodation, pairings, or schedules cannot be made out of hours.

Respond within 15 minutes. Send an email before ending every call that needs follow-up.
